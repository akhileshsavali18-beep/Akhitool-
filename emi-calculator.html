What is an EMI Calculator?

An Equated Monthly Installment (EMI) is a fixed payment amount made by a borrower to a lender at a specified date each calendar month. Equated monthly installments are used to pay off both interest and principal each month so that over a specified number of years, the loan is paid off in full.

Our free EMI Calculator helps you figure out exactly how much you need to pay per month for your home loan, car loan, personal loan, or education loan. By adjusting the loan amount, interest rate, and tenure, you can quickly assess the affordability of a loan before you apply.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is EMI calculated?

EMI is calculated using the formula: E = [P x R x (1+R)^N] / [(1+R)^N-1]. Where 'E' is the EMI, 'P' is the Principal Loan Amount, 'R' is the rate of interest calculated on a monthly basis (i.e., Annual Rate/12/100), and 'N' is the loan tenure in months.

Does the interest rate affect my EMI?

Yes, the interest rate directly impacts your EMI. A higher interest rate means a higher monthly EMI, assuming the principal amount and loan tenure remain the same.

What happens if I choose a longer loan tenure?

Choosing a longer loan tenure will decrease your monthly EMI, making it more affordable on a monthly basis. However, you will end up paying more total interest over the life of the loan.
